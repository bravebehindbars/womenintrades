Statistics To Help

Premote Women Working in Trade

Women Are Afforded Less Programing

Approximately 81,000 women are released per year from prisons. In some states there are only two programs available for women to work in while the men have multiple.

Lack Of Women In Trade

4% of workers are women in the trades industry. Tradeswomen are payed 90 cents to every mans dollar. Women entering these trades could help bridge the employment gap.

Benifits Job Training In Prison

Prisoner's have time to hone their skills, and are motivated to explore new skills. Trade oppertunities for incarcerated women help to provide the lacking work force with fresh ideas and a more diverse work environment. Not to mention the benifits for the individuals.

    Our Humble Beginnings

    This project was started in 2023 to highlight the women in Maine's correctional center.

  • November 2023

    An Online Prescence Is Born

    Two residents put their heads together along with two women from the commissioners office to create an online store in which to sell goods from inside the women's section of the correctional center.

  • December 2023

    Planning To Expand

    The residents in the Industries Department prepose their plan to create more jobs, improve space and design a buisness model to support renovations and innovations.

  • 2024

    A Second Building

    Pairing with the Women's Renentry Center the plans to build will be put in action and allow a space in which women can craft pottery, dye yarns, wood burn, laser engrave and any other project that would need a ventalated space.

Gardening

Agricultural Technologies

The Women's Green House was the pioneering act partnered with MPAC to provide seeds for the start of their seed bank, the women worked tirelessly to expand this program. They grow vegitables, friuts and flowers and are planning to winterize their green house.

  • Client: Master Gardening Program
  • Category: Agricultural Technologies

Coding

Computer Sciences

The women here in Maine have great oppertunities to work with MIT in programs like Brave Behind Bars, and Python classes.

  • Client: Coding
  • Category: Computer Sciences

Women's Welding

Maine Women Metal Workers

DOC partnered with CMCC in a joint mission to enroll women in a welding course. Vocational trades instructor Corrine Bailey is the founder of this new program featured just this year in 2023.

  • Client: Welding
  • Category: Metal Working

Engraving

Diode Laser and CNC

Featured in the Women's Center Industries building, the women have the capabilities to use a Diode Laser and CNC router. They make various products for DOC and craft and create products that will be soon sold in an online pressance as well as in stores around Maine.

  • Client: Laser & CNC
  • Category: Engraving

DTG & Sublimation

Substrate Production

Using an Epson SureColor F2100 the women are responsible for printing on t-shirts and other clothing to produce products for orders. Using Photoshop and Illustrator as well as a printing application Industires is able to create works of art on fabrics.

  • Client: DTG & Sublimation
  • Category: Substrate Production

Sewing & Embroidery

Textile Manufacturing

Using Tajmin Embrodiery machines women center industries makes hats, jacket and embroiders any kind of clothing or apparel, they have contracts through DOC and other vendors to complete products. They also sew using sewing machines to repair clothing, officers uniforms etc.

  • Client: Sewing & Embroidery
  • Category: Textile Manufacturing
